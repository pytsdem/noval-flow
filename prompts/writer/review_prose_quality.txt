Score with evidence:
1. `prose_score`: overall prose control and readability.
2. `tension_score`: whether pressure, desire, and obstacle stay active on the page.
3. `subtext_score`: whether dialogue and action carry what cannot be spoken directly.
4. `exposition_score`: how much the chapter explains instead of dramatizes. Lower is better.
5. `cliche_score`: how generic or formulaic the diction feels. Lower is better.
6. `double_duty_detail_score`: whether details also serve plot, emotion, class, power, or relationship work.
7. `scene_texture_score`: whether place, objects, labor, etiquette, weather, money, and body create presence.
8. `emotion_externalization_score`: whether feeling appears through action, pause, avoidance, body, and consequence.
9. `dialogue_subtext_score`: whether dialogue contains concealment, pressure, status, and misread.
10. `human_warmth_score`: whether the prose carries concrete cost, self-judgment, and recognizably human reaction.
11. `memorability_score`: whether the chapter leaves behind lines, images, or beats the reader can remember.
12. `pressure_authenticity_score`: whether pressure feels real because it binds body, status, money, family, institution, or choice.

Evidence rules:
1. Do not return vague comments like "the prose is ordinary" or "the emotions are weak."
2. If there is a meaningful weakness, include an `issues` item with `evidence`, `reason`, and `fix`.
3. `evidence_notes` should still be concrete, but may capture strengths as well as weaknesses.
4. Keep `rewrite_guidance` concise and actionable.
