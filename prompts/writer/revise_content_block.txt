Light revision limits:
1. Fix only the reviewed local issue; do not rewrite the plot.
2. Delete repeated setup at the block opening.
3. Merge repeated psychological explanation already covered by adjacent beats.
4. Delete repeated imagery if it adds no new action, misread, clue, cost, or relationship movement.
5. Do not add new clues, truths, people, settings, or large new paragraphs.
6. Do not change the ending hook or the handoff to the next beat.
7. Preserve useful action, dialogue, object state, and continuity with `previous_text_tail`.
8. Use `style_rules` as the story-direction style layer; when trimming repetition, preserve the style-specific pressure and chemistry it asks for.
9. 本 beat 不需要独立完成情绪闭环；不要总结关系，不要解释人物真实心意，不要替读者下结论。
10. Treat `target_length` as a hard ceiling. Revision should usually shorten the block.
11. If the block has two similar action cycles, keep the cycle with stronger relationship/cost/clue turn and delete the weaker one.
12. Replace direct explanation with visible evidence whenever possible: action, pause, object state, witness reaction, body sensation, or dialogue timing.
13. If three or more nearby sentences open with “他/她/我”, rewrite at least one of them to start from scene, object, body, or speech.
14. Keep clue or procedure language only when it immediately changes danger, choice, face, or relationship price. Otherwise compress it.
15. If the block already lands the feeling, stop. Do not add a final sentence that translates the scene into author commentary.
16. `future_beats_not_yet` marks later beats that must remain later. If current text steals their scene, clue, or ending burden, cut it back to the current beat boundary.
17. Respect `revision_brief` first. It tells you what to keep, what to cut, and what must not be introduced.
18. Use `dramatic_memory` to avoid reusing the same pain, clue, or relationship effect in slightly different wording.
19. `candidate_strategy` remains local. Use it to sharpen this beat's execution, not to change the chapter contract.
20. If a paragraph explains what readers can already infer from a gesture, object, wound, pause, or address, delete the explanation and keep the visible evidence.
21. If procedure or clue occupies more space than human cost, compress procedure first and keep the line that changes face, trust, body, or danger.
22. Preserve or restore at least one line that carries the lead's own voice, bias, or stubbornness. Do not revise the prose into generic neutrality.
23. Even when technically correct, flat pronoun-led openings should be rewritten into scene-, object-, body-, or speech-led openings when possible.
