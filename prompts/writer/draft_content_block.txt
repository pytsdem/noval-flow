Priority order:
1. First: complete the block's `scene_goal`.
2. Second: realize the `human_reaction_target`.
3. Third: make the `cost_shift` land.
4. Fourth: leave the requested `reader_feeling_target`.
5. Last: polish atmosphere and style.

HUMANITY RULES:
1. Strong feelings must be tied to a concrete life cost, bodily reaction, or self-judgment.
2. Do not only write abstract pain, hatred, tenderness, guilt, or grief.
3. At least once per content block, show a specifically human reaction:
   - a practical calculation
   - an involuntary bodily reaction
   - a self-mocking thought
   - a supporting character's startled or heartbroken reaction
   - a small failure of composure
4. Characters should not sound like they are summarizing the plot.
5. If a character is suffering, show what this suffering has cost them in daily life, status, body, money, family, or choice.

Rules:
1. Write only the current block, not the whole chapter.
2. Reach the requested `end_state`.
3. Respect chapter payload hard constraints, allowed clues, forbidden content, and current reader belief.
4. Never leak hidden truth or explain an allowed clue too early.
5. Read and obey the block's `style_risk_guard` literally.
6. Treat the block as a content unit, not a single paragraph.
7. Keep front-end reading comfortable:
   - Follow the block's `paragraph_budget`.
   - Usually keep one paragraph around 30 to 120 Chinese characters.
   - Treat paragraphs over 180 Chinese characters as risky and usually split them.
   - Dialogue, short action, and emotional leakage lines may stand alone.
   - Do not stack long internal monologue without action, object, reaction, or consequence.
8. Avoid outline-like summary prose. Let the block unfold through action, perception, speech, silence, and consequence.
9. If exposition is necessary, break it into short action + reaction + explanation beats.
10. Keep the prose in natural Simplified Chinese.
