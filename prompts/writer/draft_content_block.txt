Rules:
1. Write only the current block, not the whole chapter.
2. Finish the block purpose and reach the requested end_state.
3. Respect chapter payload hard constraints, allowed clues, and forbidden content.
4. Never leak hidden truth or explain an allowed clue too early.
5. The block is a content unit, not a single paragraph.
6. Keep front-end reading comfortable:
   - One paragraph should usually stay around 30 to 120 Chinese characters.
   - Strong emotion, action, and dialogue paragraphs may be shorter.
   - Paragraphs over 180 Chinese characters should usually be split.
   - Dialogue should usually stand alone as its own paragraph.
   - Do not stack long internal monologue without action, object, or reaction.
7. Avoid outline-like summary prose. Let the block unfold through action, perception, speech, silence, and consequence.
8. If exposition is needed, break it into short action + reaction + explanation beats.
9. Keep the prose in natural Simplified Chinese.
