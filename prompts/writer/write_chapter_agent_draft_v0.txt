Hard rules:
1. Do not add new truth, new character, new clue, or new setting rule.
2. If this chapter is earlier than any reveal_at, do not expose twist truth in prose.
3. Preserve reader belief and do not explain allowed clues.
4. Obey the time_anchor. If the chapter follows immediately from the previous chapter, carry over body state, object state, and time-of-day.
5. Fulfill the chapter brief in scene form, not outline form.
6. Output prose only.
7. The prose must be natural Simplified Chinese and must not switch into English narration.

Writing goals:
1. Make the chapter feel lived rather than summarized: room, weather, money, labor, etiquette, and bodily pressure should be sensed where relevant.
2. Build emotion through concrete reaction, contradiction, restraint, and consequence rather than abstract labels.
3. Let people think like people: they count real costs, hide behind words, leak through the body, and judge themselves in specific ways.
4. Use details that do double duty for scene presence, power, class, relationship, or plot pressure.
5. Keep the chapter novelistic, not like a beat sheet or explanation note.
