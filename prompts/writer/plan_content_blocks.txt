Beat count:
- Around 3000 Chinese chars: 3 beats
- 4000~5500 Chinese chars: 4 beats
- 5500~7000 Chinese chars: 5 beats
- Above 7000 Chinese chars: at most 6 beats
- Around 5000 Chinese chars: default 4 beats, not 6~8

Default beat schema:
- `goal`: what this beat must accomplish on page
- `start`: where it begins, based on prior beat consequence
- `turn`: the visible change, pressure rise, cost, clue movement, or relationship reprice
- `end`: the new state handed to the next beat
- `avoid`: concrete things this beat must not repeat or reveal

Compatibility output:
- Return `{{"blocks":[...]}}` using the existing content-block fields.
- Map compact fields into existing fields: `goal -> purpose/scene_goal`, `turn -> new_value/turn_type`, `end -> end_state`, `avoid -> must_not_repeat/must_hide/style_risk_guard`.
- Keep `text` empty, `status` as `draft`, `version` as `1`.
- Use stable ids like `ch_002.sc_001.b001`; normalization may renumber them.

Planning rules:
1. Every beat must add one new value; do not split one pressure into many tiny beats.
2. Adjacent beats must change cost, relationship angle, clue pressure, or next action.
3. Do not expose hidden truth beyond allowed clues.
4. Prefer scene pressure and action carriers over explanation.
5. If no clue advances, make the beat carry pressure, relationship, or cost instead.
6. Each beat needs a clear value turn: a new misread, a new choice, a new cost, a new clue function, a new relationship reprice, or a new reader question.
7. Do not plan several beats that only solve the same rule, chase the same crisis, or repeat the same action pattern.
8. For trial/adventure/workplace crisis scenes, the external task must force a relationship change; it cannot be only a process walkthrough.
9. Keep total planned `target_chars` close to the chapter target. Do not allocate room for detours or scene replays.
